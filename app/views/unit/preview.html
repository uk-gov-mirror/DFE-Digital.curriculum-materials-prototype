What is this resource?

A video of Mount Kilauea, Hawaii (an active, shield volcano) erupting in 2019.

About Mount Kilauea

Mount Kilauea is one of the worldâ€™s most active volcanoes. It is a shield volcano that makes up the southeastern side of Hawaii. Kilauea has had more than 60 recorded eruptions in the current cycle and has been erupting on a continuous basis since 1983.

On the 3 May 2018, the volcano erupted dramatically. The eruption spewed lava into residential subdivisions in the Puna district, prompting evacuations.

Teaching with this resource

Pupils apply their prior knowledge of the different types of volcanoes, focussing on the effects of volcanic eruptions.

Ask pupils to watch the video carefully, noting down as many words as they can about what they observe. Remind them of their last lesson to help them. Follow up with a discussion to check answers and test vocabulary.
