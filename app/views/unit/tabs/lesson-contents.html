 Teacher instruction

Reading

Understanding the events of the battle in chronological order

For this lesson, students need to know the events of the battle in chronological order. The teacher uses the maps on the presentation slides to remind students what led up to the battle. Students then read the Word document “description of the Battle of Hastings” either as a group taking it in turns to read aloud or independently.

printing required

Approximately 10 minutes

3

 Verbal recap

Verbal retelling of the battle

Give students slips of paper at random, but make sure the student with the first event knows who they are. Get them to read out their slip and see how smoothly the whole class can get through the story. This exercise requires participants to have understood the order of events and be alert for when their turn to read arises. Teachers can make this more competitive by making students start again if there is a pause of more than a couple of seconds between each student.

printing required

Approximately 5 minutes

4

 Card sort

Understanding why William won the Battle of Hastings

Students can work individually, in pairs or in groups for this activity. Instructions for the activity are provided on the presentation slide. Students decide which statements on the cards are irrelevant to answering the question “Why did William win the Battle of Hastings?” and discard them. Then they categorise the cards under different headings. Higher attainers can be encouraged to come up with their own category headings or think of alternatives to the one you give them.

extra prep

Approximately 15 minutes

5

 Teacher instruction

Understanding how to construct paragraphs

Show students example paragraphs and get them to verbally feed back on what they think is wrong with the first one. Then show the better paragraph, and use questioning to get students to identify what is better about this piece of writing before you show them the complete annotated version.

Approximately 5 minutes		 No alternatives

6

 Writing

Students write their own paragraphs

Students attempt their own paragraphs. Starter sentences can be displayed on the slide for students who need more support.

Approximately 10 minutes		 No alternatives

7

 Peer assessment

Students assess a partner's paragraph

Ask students to use the checklist on the slide to feed back on the work of the person next to them. They can either award a mark for each element of the checklist that is present, or write a “what went well” and an “even better if” for their partner.

Approximately 5 minutes		 No alternatives

 Whole class

Plenary

Students share their paragraphs

Ask a student to read out their paragraph and either offer feedback or get other students to offer verbal feedback.
