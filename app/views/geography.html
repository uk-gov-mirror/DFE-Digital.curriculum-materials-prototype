Year 7 geography curriculum

What is covered in year 7

The locational, topographical and meteorological knowledge developed earlier in the key stage acts as a great foundation for the knowledge and skills to be built in future years. Themes developed over the course of multiple units include:

  • Physical map features
  • the water cycle
  • climate and change
  • urbanisation and migration
  • environmental interactions
  • landscape systems

Pupils will focus on their map skills and learn to record and communicate their fieldwork using a range of methods, in different contexts, across the year.

1

Map skills6 lessons

Introducing the core map-reading concepts and map-making skills to underpin units in the rest of the year.

  • Physical map features
  • Human map features
  • Grid references
  • Compass points
  • Scale and distance
  • Latitude, longitude and projection

2

Rivers and oceans7 lessons

A physical geography unit focussed on processes involving water, but also building on learnt map skills.

  • Physical map features
  • Water cycle
  • River features (map skills)
  • What happens at estuaries?
  • Coastal erosion
  • Continental shelf
  • Oceans and currents
View and plan lessons

3

Ecosystems7 lessons

This unit links water cycle and river knowledge, map skills and human factors to explore ecosystems in general and rainforests in particular.

  • Physical map features
  • What is an ecosystem?
  • Global ecosystems
  • What's in a rainforest?
  • Where are rainforests? (map skills)
  • Humans and the rainforest
  • Do rainforests have a future?

4

Development7 lessons

Introducing the human geography concept of development, considering the factors that cause differences of development and their effects.

  • Physical map features
  • What is development?
  • Demographic transitions (map skills)
  • The development gap
  • What causes the gap
  • Trade and aid
  • South Africa and Mozambique

Africa6 lessons

Interleaving the knowledge and skills learnt throughout the year to explore the continent of Africa.

  • Physical map features
  • Countries and landmarks
  • Population and culture
  • Welcome to Lagos
  • Africa's landscape
  • The savannah nomads

A unit focused on the physical processes that create and destroy our landscape - and their effects on humans.

  • Physical map features
  • Earth's layers
  • The plates
